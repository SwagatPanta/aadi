Sanskrit | English अदित्यहृदयम् | Aditya Hridayam ततो युध्धपरिश्रान्तं समरे चिन्तयास्तिथम्। रावणं चाग्रतो दृष्ट्वा युध्धाय समुपस्थितम्॥1॥ | Then, seeing Rama exhausted and contemplating in the battlefield, and seeing Ravana ready for battle, दैवतैश्च समागम्य द्रष्टुमभ्यागतो रणम्। उपगम्य ब्रवीद्रामं अगस्त्यो भगवान् ऋषिः॥2॥ | The sage Agastya, who had come along with the gods to witness the battle, approached Rama and said: राम राम महाबाहो शृणु गुह्यं सनातनम्। येन सर्वानरीन् वत्स समरे विजयिष्यसि॥3॥ | "O Rama, O mighty-armed one, listen to this eternal secret which will help you conquer all enemies in battle. आदित्यहृदयं पुण्यं सर्वशत्रुविनाशनम्। जयावहं जपन्नित्यं अक्षयं परमं शिवम्॥4॥ | This sacred hymn dedicated to the Sun God (Aditya) will destroy all enemies and bring you victory, eternal prosperity, and supreme welfare. सर्वमंगलमांगल्यं सर्वपापप्रणाशनम्। चिन्ताशोकप्रशमनं आयुर्वर्धनमुत्तमम्॥5॥ | It bestows all auspiciousness, destroys all sins, removes anxiety and grief, and prolongs life. रश्मिमंतं समुद्धन्तं देवासुरनमस्कृतम्। पूजयस्व विवस्वन्तं भास्करं भुवनेश्वरम्॥6॥ | Worship the Sun God, who is radiant, rising, worshipped by gods and demons, and the lord of the universe. सर्वदेवात्मको ह्येषः तेजस्वि रश्मिभावनः। एष देवासुरगणां लोकान् पती गभस्तिभिः॥7॥ | He is the embodiment of all gods, the brilliant, and the one who nourishes all the worlds with his rays. एष ब्रह्मा च विष्णुः च शिवः स्कन्दः प्रजापतिः। महेंद्रः धनदः कालः यमः सोमो ह्यपां पतिः॥8॥ | He is Brahma, Vishnu, Shiva, Skanda, and Prajapati. He is Indra, Kubera, Kala, Yama, Soma, and Varuna. पितरः वसवः साध्याः अश्विनो मरुतो मनुः। वायुर्वह्निः प्रजाप्राणः ऋतुकर्ता प्रभाकरः॥9॥ | He is the Pitras, Vasus, Sadhyas, Ashvins, Maruts, and Manu. He is Vayu, Agni, the life of all beings, the maker of seasons, and the light of the world. आदित्यः सविता सूर्यः खगः पूषा गभस्तिमान्। सुवर्णसदृशो भानुः हिरण्यरेताः दिवाकरः॥10॥ | He is Aditya, Savita, Surya, the bird (Garuda), the nourisher, the radiant, golden-colored, and the creator. हरिदाश्वः सहस्रार्चिः सप्तसप्तिर्मरीचिमान्। तिमिरोन्मथनः शम्भुः त्वष्टा मार्तण्ड अंशुमान्॥11॥ | He has green horses, thousands of rays, seven horses, and is the dispeller of darkness. He is Shambhu, Tvasta, Martanda, and Ansuman. हिरण्यगर्भः शिशिरः तपनो भास्करो रविः। अग्निगर्भोऽदितेः पुत्रः शंखः शिशिरनाशनः॥12॥ | He is Hiranyagarbha, cool-rayed, the heat-giver, the brilliant, the fire-embodied, the son of Aditi, and the destroyer of cold. व्योमनाथस्तमोभेदी ऋग्यजुःसामपारगः। घनवृष्टिरपां मित्रो विंध्यवीथीप्लवंगमः॥13॥ | He is the lord of the sky, the dispeller of darkness, the knower of the Rig, Yajur, and Sama Vedas, the cause of rain, the friend of waters, and the traveler of the Vindhya range. आतपी मण्डली मृत्युः पिंगलः सर्वतापनः। कविर्विश्वो महातेजा रक्तः सर्वभवोद्भवः॥14॥ | He is the one who heats, the circular one, the bringer of death, the tawny one, the one who heats all, the poet, the universal one, the greatly radiant, the red one, and the source of all creation. नक्षत्रग्रहताराणामधिपो विश्वभावनः। तेजसामपि तेजस्वी द्वादशात्मन्नमोऽस्तु ते॥15॥ | He is the lord of stars, planets, and constellations, the creator of the universe, the brilliant one among the brilliant, and the one with twelve forms. Salutations to you! नमः पूर्वाय गिरये पश्चिमायाद्रये नमः। ज्योतिर्गणानां पतये दिनाधिपतये नमः॥16॥ | Salutations to the eastern mountain, the western mountain, the lord of the stellar bodies, and the lord of the day. जयाय जयभद्राय हर्यश्वाय नमो नमः। नमो नमः सहस्रांशो आदित्याय नमो नमः॥17॥ | Salutations to the victorious, the blessed, the one with green horses. Salutations, salutations to the thousand-rayed, to Aditya. नमः उग्राय वीराय सारंगाय नमो नमः। नमः पद्मप्रबोधाय मार्तण्डाय नमो नमः॥18॥ | Salutations to the fierce one, the valiant one, the one with a horse named Saranga. Salutations to the one who awakens the lotus, to Martanda. ब्रह्मेशानाच्युताय सुर्यायादित्यवर्चसे। भास्वते सर्वभक्षाय रौद्राय वपुषे नमः॥19॥ | Salutations to Brahma, Ishana, Achyuta, Surya, the radiant Aditya, the brilliant, the all-consuming, and the one with a fierce form. तमोघ्नाय हिमघ्नाय शत्रुघ्नायामितात्मने। कृतघ्नघ्नाय देवाय ज्योतिषां पतये नमः॥20॥ | Salutations to the dispeller of darkness, the destroyer of cold, the destroyer of enemies, the infinite soul, the destroyer of the ungrateful, the god, and the lord of lights. तप्तचामीकराभाय हरये विश्वकर्मणे। नमस्तमोऽभिनिघ्नाय रुचये लोकसाक्षिणे॥21॥ | Salutations to the one who shines like molten gold, the remover of darkness, the maker of the universe, the dispeller of darkness, the one with bright rays, and the witness of the world. नाशयत्येष वै भूतं तदेव सृजति प्रभुः। पायत्येष तपत्येष वर्षत्येष गभस्तिभिः॥22॥ | He indeed destroys all that exists, creates it again, protects it, heats it, and causes it to rain with his rays. एष सुप्तेषु जागर्ति भूतेषु परिनिष्ठितः। एष एवाग्निहोत्रं च फलं चैवाग्निहोत्रिणाम्॥23॥ | He remains awake when all beings sleep. He alone is the fire sacrifice and the fruit of the fire sacrifice. वेदाश्च क्रतवश्चैव क्रतुष्चैव फलोद्भवः। यानी कृत्यानि लोकेषु सर्व एष रविः प्रभुः॥24॥ | He is the Vedas, the sacrifices, and the fruit of the sacrifices. He is all the actions in the world. एनमापत्सु कृच्छ्रेषु कान्तारेषु भयेषु च। कीर्तयन् पुरुषः कश्चिन्नावसीदति राघव॥25॥ | By chanting his name, a person does not suffer in difficult situations, in forests, or in fears. पूजयस्वैनमेकाग्रो देवदेवं जगत्पतिम्। एतत् त्रिगुणितं जप्त्वा युध्देषु विजयिष्यसि॥26॥ | Worship him with a focused mind, the god of gods, the lord of the universe. By chanting this thrice, you will win in battles. अस्मिन्क्षणे महाबाहो रावणं त्वं वधिष्यसि। एवमुक्त्वा तदाऽगस्त्यो जगाम च यथागतम्॥27॥ | At this moment, O mighty-armed one, you will slay Ravana. Saying this, the sage Agastya went back as he came. एतच्छ्रुत्वा महातेजा नष्टशोकोऽभवत् तदा। धारयामास सुप्रीतो राघवः प्रयतात्मवान्॥28॥ | Hearing this, the greatly radiant Rama, free from sorrow, carried it out with a pleased and concentrated mind. आदित्यं प्रेक्ष्य जप्त्वा तु परं हर्षमवाप्तवान्। त्रिराचम्य शुचिर्भूत्वा धनुरादाय वीर्यवान्॥29॥ | Looking at Aditya and chanting it, he attained great joy. After sipping water thrice and purifying himself, he took his bow with great strength. रावणं प्रेक्ष्य हृष्टात्मा युध्दाय समुपागमत्। सर्वयत्नेन महता वधे तस्य धृतोऽभवत्॥30॥ | Seeing Ravana, he approached for battle with a delighted mind. He resolved with great effort to slay him. अथ रविरवदन्निरीक्ष्य रामं मुदितमनाः परं हर्षम्। राघवः प्रस्थितः रावणं हन्तुम्॥31॥ | Then, seeing Rama with a joyful mind, the Sun God, filled with great delight, looked upon him. Raghava (Rama) set out to slay Ravana.
